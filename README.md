Melted steel is poured into the bucket and heated to the required temperature with graphite electrodes, which are installed in the bucket's lid. Slag is removed from the alloy (this process is desulfurization), the chemical composition is corrected by adding impurities, and samples are selected. Steel is alloyed by changing its composition by feeding pieces of the alloy from a bunker for powder materials or wire through a special trial-apparatus (from English "tribe" - "mass"). Before introducing alloying additives for the first time, the temperature of the steel is measured and its chemical analysis is performed. Then the temperature is raised for a few minutes, alloying materials are added, and the alloy is blown with inert gas. Then it is mixed again and measurements are taken again. Such a cycle is repeated until the target chemical composition and optimal melting temperature are achieved. Then the melted steel is sent for metal finishing or goes into a continuous casting machine. From there, the finished product is produced in the form of slabs. |
